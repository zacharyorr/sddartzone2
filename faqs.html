Where Should We Host?

Our mission is to provide the full experience of a Nerf Party without the hassle of traveling to a far-away arena. Instead, we bring the party to you! We can host in most spaces, (20ft x 40ft recommended) from large backyards, to gyms, to local parks. All you need to do is let us know the venue, and we will tailor our services, from the blasters we bring, to the size and number of inflatables we bring to your needs.

What are Your Reservation Policies?

In order to reserve a timeslot and party, contact us through our form in the booking page! We will contact you to confirm your reservation and send you an invoice. Your timeslot will not be reserved until you make the mandatory $100 deposit. The deposit is fully refundable up to two days before the time of your party, and 50% refundable until 24 hours before your party. At that point, the deposit will be lost. If you have a change in location for the party, there is no relocation fee, just let us know at least a few hours before!

What About Bad Weather?

We understand that the weather just doesn't cooperate sometimes. As such, we offer full refunds up to a day before the party due to weather, with at least a 20% chance of rain during the party time. If your party is rained out during the party, we will offer refunds on a case by case basis, based on how much of the party was lost. Likewise, if the party becomes dangerously hot, we will discuss cancellations and refunds for heat on a case by case basis. However, we understand that heat can be dangerous, and we take it very seriously.

Are Your Activities Safe?

While Nerf guns are extremely safe toys, we still work to ensure you and your party remain safe and secure. We provide all required vests and safety goggles, of which the goggles must be worn at all times to play. In the event of an injury, please refer to our waiver. A signed waiver is required by all participants in order to play. Our parties are also fully insured up to $2,000,000, so rest assured, you will be covered.

What do You Play?

In our Nerf parties, we provide many different ways to play, from individual, to team-based: everything we need to make sure your party never gets old. Below is a list of "Gamemodes" that we set up during our parties.

  • Attack and Defend: In this gamemode, one team must defend an objective (a flag) from the enemy team for a certain amount of time. Each player has one life, and if shot, is out for the round. The defenders win the game by eliminating the attackers or by outlasting the timer. The attackers win the game by eliminating all the defenders or capturing their flag.
  • Team Deathmatch: In this gamemode, two teams face off on opposite ends of the field. Each player has one life, and the teams both try to eliminate all members of the other team. The last team standing wins!
  • Capture the Flag: In this gamemode, two teams face off on opposite ends of the field. Each team has one flag. A team wins by "capturing" the other team's flag and bringing it to their own base. Each player has unlimited lives, but if shot, must wait on the sidelines for thirty seconds.
  • Fortnite: In this gamemode, individuals pair up in groups of two or four. Each team can use a certain number of inflatables to "build" a base, attacking other bases when their building time is up. The last team standing wins.
  • Zombies: In this gamemode, all but two individuals are humans; the other two are zombies, who do not have Nerf guns. When the game starts, the zombies attempt to "infect" the humans by tagging them. "Zombies" have unlimited lives, but can only respawn after 10 seconds outside the battlefield. The game ends when all players have been infected.
  • Target Practice: In this gamemode, two teams line up on opposite ends of the arena. The two teams are not allowed past a center-line, only staying on their own half. From their own half, it is their goal to shoot three targets on the opposite side of the map. Players have unlimited lives, but must wait 30 seconds before they can respawn. The first team to shoot all of the enemy's targets wins.
  • Hunger Games:In this gamemode, teams of two start in different areas of the field. Nerf guns are hidden all around the field, and the players must find them before battling it out with their friends! The last team standing wins the Hunger Games!
  • Empty Hands Deathmatch: In this gamemode, two teams again start on opposite ends of the field. However, unlike before, each team has a certain number of lives. Whenever a player dies, one life is removed from their team's count. The team that runs out of lives loses!
